# Strategic Communications Template


_Turn leadership ideas into meaningful, memorable communication—without losing clarity, credibility, or context._

***

## ❓ Why This Template Exists


> Strategy dies when it becomes a slogan.\
> Most comms aren’t strategic. They’re summaries wearing a suit.

Executives often think they’ve communicated clearly just because they’ve said something once—on a slide, in a town hall, or in a 6-paragraph email.

The truth?

People need strategy to be:

* Contextualized → “Why now?”
* Localized → “How does this affect _my_ team?”
* Actionable → “What do I do differently starting tomorrow?”

This template helps you **translate strategy into sense**.

***

## ✍️ The Template


Use this for quarterly updates, town hall scripts, all-hands recaps, or cross-functional strategy docs.

```
##  The Core Message

[Boil it down: What does the org want to do? In plain English.]

##  Why This, Why Now?

[Explain what triggered this shift—data, insight, market tension.]

##  What It Reflects About Us

[Show how this decision fits your org’s values or goals.]

##  Who This Affects (And How)

[Be honest. Name the teams or roles. List real implications.]

## ️ Whats Changing and Whats Not

[Don’t just focus on what’s new. Reassure stability.]

##  What Success Looks Like

[Define success in real terms—not just vision statements.]

##  What We Need From You

[Make the ask specific, not motivational.]

##  What to Say If Youre Asked

[Give teams language to answer “Why?” internally.]
```

***

##  Real-World Example


Let’s say your company is shifting from project-based billing to a subscription-based model.

***

### ❌ Typical Strategic Memo:


> “As we move into 2025, we’re embracing a more scalable and future-forward pricing model designed to serve our customers with increased agility.”

🥴 Translation: “No one knows what this means.”

***

### ✅ Using the Strategic Comms Template:


```
##  The Core Message  

We’re moving from one-time project billing to a subscription-based model for all core services.

##  Why This, Why Now?  

Most of our clients now ask for continuity and predictability.  
Our current model makes forecasting hard—on both sides.

##  What It Reflects About Us  

We’ve always believed in long-term partnerships.  
This move supports that, and aligns us with industry standards.

##  Who This Affects (And How)  

- Sales will shift to renewal-based contracts  
- Finance will adjust cashflow forecasting  
- CS teams will work on ongoing support models instead of hand-offs

## ️ Whats Changing and Whats Not  

Changing: How we bill, how we renew, how we measure success  
Not Changing: Our services, our SLAs, and our core mission

##  What Success Looks Like  

By Q2, 80% of clients should be on the new model without service disruption.  
CSAT scores and renewal rates will guide us.

##  What We Need From You  

Leads: Start using the new quoting tool this week.  
All teams: Begin identifying clients who might need extra onboarding help.

##  What to Say If Youre Asked  

“Our shift to subscriptions helps clients get more consistent support, and it gives us a clearer long-term roadmap together.”
```

***

##  When To Use This Template


* When you're rolling out **org-wide strategic shifts**
* When you need teams to **align across functions**
* When leadership wants to share _why_ decisions were made—not just what was decided
* When confusion and speculation would create more resistance than the change itself

***

##  Why It Works


| Section                   | Why It Helps                                  |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| “The Core Message”        | Gives everyone one sentence to align around   |
| “Why This, Why Now?”      | Creates urgency without panic                 |
| “What It Reflects…”       | Builds emotional alignment to identity        |
| “Who This Affects…”       | Prevents vague promises from hiding the truth |
| “What Success Looks Like” | Makes strategy tangible and trackable         |
| “What To Say…”            | Helps managers echo the message downstream    |

***

## ✅ TL;DR


You don’t need to “sound strategic.”\
You need to **translate the strategy into something people can carry**.

Use this when:

* There’s risk of misalignment or misinterpretation
* You need every team to get the same version of the truth
* You want action—not just applause
