# Content Enablement Template

_A practical scaffold for writing useful, usable, and relevant support content when change is in motion_

***

## 🧭 Why This Template Exists

> Most enablement content tries to teach too much, too soon, with too little connection.

People don’t reject learning.\
They reject **irrelevant explanations** at the wrong time in the wrong format.

Here’s what usually happens:

* A new dashboard rolls out → Enablement team sends a 5-slide deck
* Users skim it (if at all) → Adoption stalls → More training is forced
* Feedback says “It’s confusing,” but no one knows _where_ or _why_

The missing piece?\
Enablement that’s written **for the moment they get stuck**—not just the moment you launched.

***

## ✍️ The Template

Use this structure when creating any **just-in-time content** (emails, help articles, walkthroughs, UI microcopy):

```
## 🧭 What Just Changed
[Write in one sentence. Be specific, not dramatic.]

## 👀 What You'll Notice
[List 2–3 behavior-level changes they’ll observe. Use real language.]

## 😕 What Might Feel Off
[Acknowledge friction. Preempt resistance.]

## ✅ What To Try Instead
[Offer 1–2 steps they can take. Actionable. Friction-aware.]

## 🧠 What It’s Meant to Improve
[Connect back to purpose without hype.]

## 🤔 What To Do If You’re Still Confused
[Link to 1 next step. Set emotional tone: safe to not know.]
```

***

## 🔍 Real-World Example

Let’s say a product team introduces a new tagging system.

***

### ❌ Typical Content:

> “We’re excited to introduce Smart Tags—a new way to auto-categorize your content! Learn more in our 10-minute video walkthrough.”

🙈 Too long. Too vague. No bridge to reality.

***

### ✅ Enablement Using This Template:

```
## 🧭 What Just Changed  
We replaced manual tags with Smart Tags that auto-suggest based on what you’re writing.

## 👀 What You'll Notice  
- Your usual tag list is gone  
- A small “Suggested” chip appears after saving

## 😕 What Might Feel Off  
You won’t find your old custom tags anymore—and suggestions may feel weird at first.

## ✅ What To Try Instead  
- Start typing like you normally would  
- If a suggestion looks off, hit “Edit Tags” to tweak it

## 🧠 What It’s Meant to Improve  
This helps keep content consistent across teams—especially for new users.

## 🤔 What To Do If You’re Still Confused  
Ping us in #smart-tags-support or watch the 90-second walkthrough [here].
```

***

## 🛠️ When To Use This Template

Use this structure:

* After any release that changes a **workflow, language, or interface**
* When writing follow-ups that aim to **reduce user hesitation**
* When early adoption data shows **people are unsure but not asking**
* To build micro-copy or short-form comms inside apps, tools, or emails

***

## 🧠 Why It Works

| Element                               | Why It Helps                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| “What Just Changed”                   | Prevents assumption that users already know |
| “What You'll Notice”                  | Anchors to lived behavior, not features     |
| “What Might Feel Off”                 | Builds trust through acknowledgement        |
| “What To Try Instead”                 | Encourages safe experimentation             |
| “What It’s Meant to Improve”          | Reconnects to purpose (without hype)        |
| “What To Do If You're Still Confused” | Provides permission and safe next step      |

***

## ✅ TL;DR

This isn’t just a content structure.\
It’s a **clarity ritual**.

Use this whenever you’re writing enablement for something that:

* Might feel unfamiliar
* Changes how people interact
* Requires reflection before instruction

Your content doesn’t need to say everything.\
It just needs to say what **matters at the moment of confusion**.
