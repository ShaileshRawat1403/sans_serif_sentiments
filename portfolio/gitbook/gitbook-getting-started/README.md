# 🚀 Getting Started with GitBook

Use this guide to create your first documentation space in GitBook, add a page, and publish it.  
This is the next step after configuring your account and workspace.

---

## 📌 Overview

A **space** in GitBook is a standalone project that contains one or more pages of documentation.  
Spaces can be public or private, and they can be structured into sections and subpages for clarity.

This guide helps you create a new space and publish your first document.

---

## ✅ Prerequisites

- A GitBook account  
- Access to your workspace  
- Editor or Admin role assigned  

> 🔄 If you haven’t completed setup, [follow the Setup Guide →](./gitbook-setup-guide.md)

---

## 📁 Create a New Space

- From your workspace dashboard, locate the **+ New space** button  
- Click it to open the space creation modal  
- Provide a name for your space (e.g., `Product Docs` or `API Reference`)  
- Choose visibility:
  - **Private** (default): Only visible to workspace members  
  - **Public**: Anyone with the link can view it  
- Click **Create**

> 🖼️  
> ![New space creation screen](images/gitbook-create-space-modal.png)  
> *Name your documentation space and select visibility settings.*

---

## 📄 Add Your First Page

- Your space will open with a blank sidebar and welcome prompt  
- Click **“+ New page”**  
- Enter a page title (e.g., `Welcome`, `Start Here`, or `Overview`)  
- Begin writing using:
  - The **Block editor** (default) — use `/` to insert elements  
  - Or switch to **Markdown mode** if preferred

> 🖼️  
> ![New page interface in GitBook](images/gitbook-add-page.png)  
> *Use the editor to add text, headings, images, and links.*

---

## 🌍 Publish Your Page

- Click **Publish** in the top-right corner  
- Confirm your space’s visibility (Public or Private)  
- GitBook may prompt you to "Start your trial" if this is your first public space  
- Click **Confirm publish** or select **Publish later** to stay in draft mode

> 🖼️  
> ![Publish confirmation with trial notice](images/gitbook-publish-options.png)  
> *Choose to publish now or defer publishing until later.*

---

## ✅ Outcome

After completing this guide, you will have:

- Created a new space in your workspace  
- Added and edited your first documentation page  
- Chosen whether to publish your space publicly or keep it private
