#  Project Manifesto: Migration to JIRA  

**Title**: Aligning Projects, People, and Processes

---

##  Project Summary


This initiative aims to migrate all project and task management workflows from decentralized tools (Trello, Excel, Basecamp, emails) to **JIRA**, enabling scalable, auditable, and standardized project tracking across the organization.

While the technical switch is manageable, the core challenge is **behavioral** — ensuring every team understands the why, how, and when of this migration, and feels equipped to adopt JIRA with minimal friction.

---

##  Business Problem


- Fragmented task management across tools creates silos, miscommunication, and data inconsistency  
- Leadership lacks unified visibility into project status across teams  
- Project managers and support teams struggle with redundant updates, manual tracking, and inconsistent workflows

---

##  Project Objectives


- Establish JIRA as the **single source of truth** for work management  
- Enable cross-team visibility, standardization, and reporting at scale  
- Reduce tool fatigue and training overhead by streamlining workflows  
- Align stakeholders through clear, continuous, human-centered communication

---

##  Strategic Approach


1. **Communications First**: Announce the why before the what — to reduce anxiety  
2. **Segmented Messaging**: Tailor messages by role (Dev, PM, Ops, Exec)  
3. **Docs-as-Enablement**: Every change comes with a clear how-to or explainer  
4. **Visible Support**: Slack channel, weekly drop-ins, and real-time FAQs  
5. **Feedback-Driven Iteration**: Surveys, retrospectives, and refinements
