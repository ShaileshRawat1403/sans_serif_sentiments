So far, we've covered the philosophy and mindset.  
This file is about **practice** — how to use systems thinking in real work:

- 📄 Writing documentation

- 📢 Communicating complex ideas

Let’s walk through the tools and loops you can apply immediately.

---

## 🌀 The FnF Writing Loop: Flavor + Function

At the heart of our system is a **writing rhythm**.  
One that makes things feel human, structured, and usable.

We call it:

### 🎯 Friction → Bridge → Evidence → Implication → Action → Look Ahead

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Friction** | Start with the problem or tension the reader feels |
| **Bridge** | Acknowledge that pain and build a human connection |
| **Evidence** | Share real-world insight, fact, analogy, or system |
| **Implication** | Show why it matters now, and what’s at stake |
| **Action** | Offer a resolution, tool, technique, or decision point |
| **Look Ahead** | Hint at broader context or deeper future questions |

> It’s not just storytelling.  
> It’s structured, trustworthy, tension-driven clarity.

## 🪜 Prompting as Mental Scaffolding

Every good prompt is built like a **micro-document**.

It needs:
- A purpose
- A role or tone
- Context clues
- Boundaries or examples
- Desired style or structure
- A test loop for regeneration

We treat prompts like **modular units of structured communication** — not magic spells.

---

## 🔀 The Modular Content Lens

Whether writing docs, blogs, or AI interactions — all content is improved when seen as:

- **Chunks** (not walls of text)
- **Connected** (via hyperlinks or hierarchy)
- **Contextual** (with scannable headings and cues)
- **Composable** (reusable in other formats or prompts)

Ask yourself:
- Can this stand alone?
- Can this plug into something else?
- Can it be reused with minimal editing?

If yes — it’s modular.
