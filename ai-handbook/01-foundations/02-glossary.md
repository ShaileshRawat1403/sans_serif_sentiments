# 📘 Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines common terms used across this handbook to ensure clarity, consistency, and shared understanding between writers, thinkers, engineers, and collaborators.

---

## 🧠 Cognitive & Prompting Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Mental Model** | The internal framework a person uses to understand and interact with systems, tasks, or tools. |
| **Cognitive Load** | The total amount of mental effort being used in the working memory — crucial in prompt design. |
| **Thinking Loop** | A structure in which prompts are designed to generate, reflect, and regenerate ideas iteratively. |
| **Prompt Engineering** | The practice of crafting input queries that influence and optimize the behavior of language models. |
| **Thought Pattern** | Recurring mental pathways or logic flows used in prompting or processing output. |

---

## 🛠 Prompt Mechanics & Structure

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Instructional Prompt** | A prompt that gives clear commands or tasks to the AI (e.g., “Summarize this paragraph”). |
| **Context Window** | The maximum number of tokens (words and symbols) the model can consider at once. |
| **Token** | A chunk of text — usually a few characters or a word — used by the AI for processing. |
| **Chain-of-Thought** | A prompting technique where the AI is guided to reason through steps before answering. |
| **Zero-shot / Few-shot** | Prompt styles that provide no examples or limited examples, respectively. |

---

## 🧱 Documentation & Writing Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Modular Documentation** | A method of organizing content into self-contained, reusable chunks that build on one another. |
| **Style Guide** | A set of principles and formatting rules that ensure consistent tone, language, and presentation. |
| **FnF (Flavor and Function)** | Our unique writing approach combining philosophical insight with practical structure. |
| **Systems & Sentences** | A foundational framework that sees writing as structural thought, not just surface communication. |
| **Glossary** | (This document) — A shared language bank that reduces ambiguity and speeds up understanding. |

---

## ⚖️ Governance, Bias & Risk

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Model Hallucination** | When an AI generates plausible-sounding but factually incorrect or misleading information. |
| **Bias Vector** | The encoded preferences or prejudices in a model, often due to its training data. |
| **Risk Surface** | The set of ways a system can be exploited or misinterpreted, either technically or socially. |
| **Prompt Injection** | A technique where malicious or hidden instructions are embedded in prompts or data. |
