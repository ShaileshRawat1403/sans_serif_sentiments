# 05-action.md

_“What should the reader do, shift, or notice next?”_  
Part of the **Flavor and Function (FnF)** writing framework.

---

## ✅ What Is “Action” in FnF?

In FnF, **Action** isn’t always a literal task.  
It’s the **reader’s next step** — mental, emotional, or behavioral.

It answers:

- Now that I know this… what do I _do_?
- What should change?
- Where does my attention go next?

The action is where **content becomes consequential**.

---

## 🔧 Why It Matters

Even powerful ideas fall flat if they don’t land with direction.

**Action** helps the reader:

- Feel a sense of resolution
- Know where to go or what to explore
- Shift mindset, not just accumulate info
- Connect insight to habit or behavior

> If your piece ends with _“so what?”_ — it’s not done yet.

---

## 🧪 How to Use It

You don’t always need to prescribe.  
Sometimes, a **nudge** or a **reflection hook** is enough.

Depending on your content type:

| Use Case         | Action Style Example                                                  |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Instructional    | “Try this 2-minute checklist before your next content draft.”         |
| Philosophical    | “What if the real scarcity isn’t attention, but intention?”           |
| UX/Product       | “Test this microcopy with 3 different user moods.”                    |
| Culture/Behavior | “Next time you scroll, ask yourself: Who benefits from this post?”    |
| Technical        | “Use this Git command to verify version history before merging.”      |

---

## ✍️ Examples

**From Insight to Action**

> **Implication**: “Trust isn’t just broken — it’s being gamified.”
>
> **Action**: Watch the next ad you see. Ask: _Is it solving a problem, or performing empathy?_

---

> **Implication**: “Your internal docs are readable — but not usable.”
>
> **Action**: Pick one doc. Give it to a new joiner. Can they finish the task without asking you anything?

---

> **Implication**: “AI tools are fast, but the user’s thinking is still the bottleneck.”
>
> **Action**: Reframe your next prompt using the ‘who, why, and when’ model — before the ‘what.’

---

## 🔍 Watch For

| Weak Action                         | Stronger Revision                               |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| “And that’s the takeaway.”         | Leave the reader with **something to try.**      |
| “Let that sink in.”                | Invite them to **observe, reflect, or test.**    |
| “Be aware.”                        | Awareness alone isn’t change. Suggest a shift.   |
| “That’s the lesson.”               | Offer a **next move**, not just a final word.    |

---

## 🔁 Tips for Great Action Hooks

- Use verbs — even for thinking: _“Reflect on...”_, _“Test by...”_, _“Try flipping...”_
- Avoid generic calls like “just do it”
- Tie the action to the **core insight**, not just a tool or tip
- If you want depth: pose a question, not a command

---

## ✅ Checklist

Before closing your piece:

- [ ] Did I leave the reader somewhere — not nowhere?
- [ ] Is the action rooted in the core idea, not tacked on?
- [ ] Could the reader _use_ this, _notice_ something new, or _do_ something differently?

---

> **Insight without direction fades.**  
> **Action gives it legs.**
