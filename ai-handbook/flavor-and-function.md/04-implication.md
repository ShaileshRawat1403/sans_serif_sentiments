# 04-implication.md

_“Why does it matter, and what does it change?”_  
Part of the **Flavor and Function (FnF)** writing framework.

---

## ✳️ What Is “Implication” in FnF?

In FnF, **Implication** is the meaning ripple — the idea that goes beyond the obvious.

It asks:

- So what?
- Why should the reader care?
- What will this shift, break, prove, or unlock?

It’s **not** about being preachy.  
It’s about showing the **invisible consequences** or connections that the main point brings.

---

## 🧠 Why It Matters

People don’t remember facts — they remember **what facts meant**.

Implication helps you:

- Trigger reflection
- Expose unseen patterns
- Tie relevance to real life
- Seed insight that lingers

Without this layer, your writing may feel **accurate but empty**.

---

## 🧪 How to Use It

After delivering your **evidence**, ask:

> “If this is true… what does it change?”

Use implication to connect your point to:

- A wider system
- A shift in belief
- An emotional cost or consequence
- A common misconception or trap
- A real-world ripple (e.g., trust, policy, behavior)

---

## ✍️ Examples

**Example A – Technical Context**

> **Evidence**: “Users often skip onboarding screens within 8 seconds.”
>
> **Implication**: This means your most important UX guidance is likely ignored. You’re not losing users — you’re failing to meet them when it matters.

---

**Example B – Social Insight**

> **Evidence**: “People read more headlines than articles.”
>
> **Implication**: Information today isn’t consumed — it’s performed. We’re not educating minds; we’re curating impressions.

---

**Example C – Personal Reflection**

> **Evidence**: “The average person touches their phone 2,617 times a day.”
>
> **Implication**: Maybe our attention isn’t being stolen — maybe we’re giving it away without asking why.

---

## 🔍 Watch For

| Weak Implication                    | Stronger Approach                                 |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| “This is bad.”                     | Show **why it matters now**, and to whom.         |
| “This proves we’re right.”         | Show **what this changes** or challenges.         |
| “So we should be careful.”         | Show **what happens if we aren’t.**               |
| “It connects to everything.”       | Point out **one specific ripple or consequence.** |

---

## ✅ Checklist

Before you move on to the **Action** part:

- [ ] Did you expose a deeper tension or shift?
- [ ] Did you tie the fact to something human or systemic?
- [ ] Does it spark “hmm…” or “oh…” in the reader?

---

> **Remember:**  
> Evidence shows.  
> **Implication transforms.**
