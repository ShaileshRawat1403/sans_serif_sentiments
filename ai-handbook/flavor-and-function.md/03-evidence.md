# 03 – Evidence

_“If your story doesn’t have proof, it’s just a performance.”_

In the Flavor and Function (FnF) model, **Evidence** is the point where intuition meets verification. You’ve made a claim (Hook), explained its relevance (Bridge), and now the reader needs reason to believe you.

Evidence doesn’t always mean data or citations. It can be:

- Real-world examples
- Cultural references
- Case studies
- User behavior patterns
- Screenshots, tables, quotes, analogies

The goal is to make your idea **feel real** — not just read well.

---

## Why This Step Matters

We live in a world drowning in “takes.”  
What stands out isn’t just voice — it’s **validity**.

> "Content without evidence is like a table with no legs. Balanced, maybe. But not useful."

Evidence gives your writing **gravity**. It shows you did the work. It makes your insight not just sound clever, but **feel earned**.

---

## Formats You Can Use as Evidence

| Format Type         | Use Case                                                        | Example                                                       |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| ✅ Statistic         | To back a claim with numbers                                    | “Studies show 68% of users never read past the first screen.” |
| ✅ Analogy           | To simplify or reframe an idea                                 | “Using AI without training is like hiring a chef who hates food.” |
| ✅ Quote             | To bring authority or relatability                             | “As Paul Graham said, 'Keep your identity small.'”            |
| ✅ Observation       | When patterns are more powerful than data                      | “Most people skim docs. But they never forget error messages.”|
| ✅ Use Case          | Demonstrate it in context                                       | “In Notion, templates reduce time by 40% for repetitive tasks.”|
| ✅ Screenshot/Image  | Visually anchor your claim                                     | Insert UI feedback or before/after interface designs          |

---

## Good vs Lazy Evidence

Let’s look at a few ❌ vs ✅ examples to sharpen your lens:

| ❌ Lazy Evidence                                                                 | ✅ Strong Evidence                                                                 |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| “People don’t read docs.”                                                      | “A Nielsen Norman study found users read only 20–28% of text on a typical page.” |
| “Everyone loves dark mode.”                                                    | “GitHub’s dark mode announcement saw a 30% increase in user engagement that week.”|
| “We’ve all been there.”                                                        | “Most writers have experienced the ‘final_final_v3’ doc. That’s not a file. It’s a feeling.”|

---

## Evidence as a Trust Builder

Your goal isn’t just to be right — it’s to be **believable**. That’s what makes your message repeatable, shareable, and respected.

Think of this stage as **teaching without preaching**.

Ask yourself:

- Did I give the reader something to anchor the idea?
- Will they walk away able to retell this with confidence?
- Can this point stand on its own without me in the room?

---

## When Not to Use Data

Sometimes the best evidence is **not** statistical.

- When writing emotionally (use story)
- When explaining complexity (use analogy)
- When showing friction (use screenshots)
- When persuading ethically (use consequence)

> _“Not all facts feel true. And not all truth needs a number.”_

Use what fits the **flavor** of your message — and enhances its **function**.

---

## Mini Checklist: Evidence Layer

✅ Is there something real, relatable, or referenced backing your idea?  
✅ Did I choose the format of evidence intentionally (data, story, quote, visual)?  
✅ Will this make the reader _trust_ my point even if they disagree?  
✅ Can the reader reuse this example to explain it to others?
