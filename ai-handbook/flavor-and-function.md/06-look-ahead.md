# 06-look-ahead.md

_“Where does the journey lead from here?”_  
Part of the **Flavor and Function (FnF)** writing framework.

---

## 🔭 What Is “Look Ahead” in FnF?

**Look Ahead** is the final thread — it points forward.  
It leaves the reader with **curiosity**, **context**, or **continuity**.

In FnF, it’s not just about ending — it’s about **opening the next door**.

---

## 🧠 Why It Matters

Readers don’t just want closure — they want **connection**.

A strong Look Ahead helps your content:

- Feel complete, yet unfinished (in a good way)
- Anchor into a broader theme or future implication
- Show that your insight is **part of something bigger**

> The best endings are _invitations_.

---

## 🎯 How to Use It

Choose the right lens depending on what you want to achieve:

| Lens Type       | Example Prompt                                                        |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Temporal        | “This isn’t just a now problem. What happens when this scales?”        |
| Thematic        | “Today it’s AI prompts. Tomorrow it’s cognitive agency.”               |
| Personal Lens   | “If this made you pause — what else in your workflow deserves reflection?” |
| Pattern Lens    | “We’ve seen this before: every tool becomes noise before it becomes norm.” |
| Philosophical   | “Maybe the real prompt isn’t to the AI. It’s to ourselves.”            |

---

## ✍️ Examples

> **Action**: “Try writing your next internal doc as if the reader’s time was your own.”
>
> **Look Ahead**: “Now imagine if every team did that — what would change?”

---

> **Action**: “Use the ‘why-when-who’ prompt order to improve alignment.”
>
> **Look Ahead**: “And if that became the norm? Maybe AI won’t replace jobs — just bad habits.”

---

> **Action**: “Test your onboarding doc with a new hire — no explanation allowed.”
>
> **Look Ahead**: “Because if clarity isn’t self-serve, it’s not clarity at all.”

---

## ⚠️ What to Avoid

| Weak Ending                        | Strong Look Ahead Revision                         |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| “Thanks for reading.”             | “Thanks for reading — what will _you_ write next?” |
| “Hope this helped.”               | “If this helped, what else needs a second look?”   |
| “That’s it.”                      | “That’s the start. Now where do you go from here?” |

---

## 🔁 Tips for a Great Look Ahead

- Leave a door open — don’t tie everything in a neat bow
- Invite reflection, not just feedback
- Use a **provocative question** or **unexpected insight**
- Remind them: this is part of a larger story, not just a tip

---

## ✅ Checklist

Before closing the doc:

- [ ] Does the ending point to something beyond the current page?
- [ ] Did I offer a broader lens, not just another tip?
- [ ] Would this make the reader _think_ about what’s next?

---

> **Endings don’t end ideas.**  
> **They hint at what the idea could become.**
