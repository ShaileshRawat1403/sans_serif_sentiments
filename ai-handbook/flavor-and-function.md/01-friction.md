# Friction

_Part of the Flavor and Function (FnF) Writing Framework_  
Friction is the **tension point**. It identifies the discomfort, confusion, or contradiction that grabs attention — without relying on fear or clickbait.

---

## 🔍 What Is Friction?

In writing, **friction** is the relatable moment of discomfort. It’s the part where the reader says,  
“Yeah… this is exactly what’s been bothering me.”

It could be:

- A lie we tell ourselves  
- A pattern we repeat blindly  
- A broken system we normalize  
- A gap between belief and behavior  

Friction is what creates relevance — and without relevance, no one reads on.

---

## 🎯 Why It Matters

Readers don't commit to a piece of writing unless they see themselves in it.  
Friction is what mirrors the reader's reality or struggle.

> “Friction is not pain for its own sake. It's a _truth with tension_.”

This is especially powerful when:
- You want to challenge norms  
- You’re exposing manipulation  
- You’re trying to frame a subtle human contradiction  

---

## ✍️ How to Write It

1. **Name the struggle, don't explain it.**  
   Good friction doesn’t lecture — it reveals.
2. **Show the behavior gap.**  
   e.g., “We praise vulnerability — then punish honesty at work.”
3. **Start from a question or contradiction.**  
   e.g., “If AI is so smart, why do we keep getting dumb results?”

Keep it short, sharp, and felt.  
This is **not** the time to solve — only to **unsettle**.

---

## 🧾 Examples

### ❌ Weak Friction

> “Many people face issues in communication.”  
Too generic. No tension. No emotion.

### ✅ Strong Friction

> “We spend years learning to write — and then forget how to read each other.”  
Creates contradiction, irony, and immediate curiosity.

---

## 💡 Tips

- Use simple, direct sentences  
- Avoid corporate tone or disclaimers  
- Tap into common mental patterns (“We always… yet we…”)

---

_Friction is where the reader leans in — not because you pulled them, but because you pressed where it hurts (gently)._
