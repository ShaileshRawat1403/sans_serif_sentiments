# Literary Devices

---

## ✨ Introduction: How Language, Thinking, and AI Are All Connected

**What’s the idea?**  
Language isn’t just how we express thoughts. It’s how we structure systems. And literature — especially the kind that resists convention — already mimics what AI tries to do: model meaning, simulate context, handle contradiction.

**Why now?**  
Because the distance between “content” and “code” is collapsing. The people who thrive will be those who can design meaning systems — not just prompt for answers.

**How?**  
By reading like a system architect. By writing like a prompt designer. And by treating literature as a prototype lab for intelligence.

## 📝 Literary Profiles (Sample)

### 🔵 Anne Carson – Deconstructing Form, Rewiring Perception

Carson’s style disrupts traditional formats—poetry, criticism, myth all blend. Her recursive, fragmented writing teaches us how to build AI systems that embrace ambiguity, recursion, and multimodal prompts.

#### Literary Technique: **Genre Collapse**
- **FnF Flow**: Friction → Bridge → Evidence → Implication
- What: Books are prose, essay, poem, opera—at once
- AI Mapping: Multimodal generation prompts
- Prompt Example: _"Generate a poetic commentary on this data visualization."_

#### Literary Technique: **Recursion**
- **FnF Flow**: Bridge → Evidence → Action → Look Ahead
- AI Mapping: Chain-of-thought prompting
- Prompt Example: _"Write a reflection, revise it skeptically, then synthesize."_

#### Literary Technique: **Discomfort**
- **FnF Flow**: Friction → Evidence → Implication
- AI Mapping: Adversarial prompting, critical response
- Prompt Example: _"Argue for the unpopular version of a popular idea."_

---

### 🟠 Ali Smith – Playing with Time, Shaping Reality

Smith’s work makes time and structure fluid. Her novels are conversational, layered, and open-ended — like multi-agent dialogues in AI.

#### Literary Technique: **Temporal Play**
- **FnF Flow**: Friction → Bridge → Implication → Look Ahead
- AI Mapping: Non-linear generation, time-aware context
- Prompt Example: _"Write a story that starts at the end and moves backwards."_

#### Literary Technique: **Structure Variance**
- **FnF Flow**: Bridge → Evidence → Action
- AI Mapping: Adaptive schemas, format-flexible generation
- Prompt Example: _"Reformat this summary into a poem, then into a Q&A."_

#### Literary Technique: **Dialogue / Ambiguity**
- **FnF Flow**: Friction → Evidence → Implication → Action
- AI Mapping: Multi-agent simulation, interpretability design
- Prompt Example: _"Debate an ethical dilemma with no clear resolution."_

This is not about romanticizing literature. It’s about operationalizing it.
