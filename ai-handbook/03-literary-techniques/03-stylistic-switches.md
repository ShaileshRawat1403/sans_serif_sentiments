# Stylistic Mode Switches for Prompt Design

---

## 🎯 Purpose of This Section

This section shows you how to apply **stylistic modes** — like satire, surrealism, minimalism, gothic, and more — in AI prompting. These aren’t just artistic choices. They’re **cognitive filters** that change how meaning is shaped and understood.

Think of this section as your **style-switchboard** for expressive, intentional, and surprising outputs.

---

## ✍️ What Are Stylistic Modes?

Stylistic modes are **literary and artistic “lenses”** that alter the tone, form, or logic of communication. They influence:

- **What’s emphasized** (emotion vs. fact vs. absurdity)
- **How reality is portrayed** (clear vs. fragmented vs. dreamlike)
- **Reader reaction** (shock, empathy, introspection, laughter)

When used in prompts, these styles tell AI **how** to think — not just **what** to say.

---

## 🔄 Format Pattern

To use any style, add this kind of instruction to your prompt:

> “Respond in the style of [X]”  
> “Rewrite this as a [style] interpretation”  
> “Generate a [style] version of this story/argument”

Always test tone + output fit.

---

## 🧪 Examples of Style-Based Prompting

### 🎭 Satire

**What:** Uses irony, exaggeration, and wit to critique.  
**Prompt Example:**
```prompt
“Rewrite this corporate press release as a dark satire, exposing the hypocrisy behind the language.”
```
**Why It Works:** Reveals contradictions, exposes bias, highlights absurdities.

---

### 🌫️ Surrealism

**What:** Blends dream logic, symbolism, and irrational sequences.  
**Prompt Example:**
```prompt
“Describe a morning routine using surreal imagery and fragmented consciousness — like a dream half-remembered.”
```
**Why It Works:** Breaks patterns, stimulates imagination, useful in creative ideation.

---

### 📉 Minimalism

**What:** Strips language to essentials — no flair, only function.  
**Prompt Example:**
```prompt
“Summarize this in 3 short lines. Remove all fluff. Say only what matters.”
```
**Why It Works:** Sharpens clarity, forces precision, helps with low-bandwidth communication.

---

### 🦇 Gothic

**What:** Emphasizes decay, darkness, psychological depth, and moral ambiguity.  
**Prompt Example:**
```prompt
“Rewrite this onboarding email as a gothic horror — make it feel like the user is entering a haunted system.”
```
**Why It Works:** Adds dramatic tension, plays with atmosphere, triggers emotional resonance.

---

### 🧠 Analytical/Academic

**What:** Formal, structured, citation-heavy tone.  
**Prompt Example:**
```prompt
“Write this opinion as an academic abstract with references and logical justification.”
```
**Why It Works:** Builds authority, useful for professional/technical output.

---

## 🧠 Style Matters: Not Just What, But _How_

| Style       | Emotion Evoked     | Use Case                                     |
|-------------|--------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Satire      | Discomfort / Humor | Corporate critique, exposing hypocrisy       |
| Surrealism  | Wonder / Confusion | Creative writing, art prompts, ideation      |
| Minimalism  | Clarity / Brevity  | UX writing, messaging, signal extraction     |
| Gothic      | Dread / Awe        | Storytelling, dark interfaces, game writing  |
| Academic    | Logic / Trust      | Reports, whitepapers, policy documentation   |

---

## 🔧 FnF Format Summary

Each style can follow the **Flavor & Function** scaffold:

- **Friction:** What’s missing without the style?
- **Bridge:** Why this mode unlocks insight
- **Evidence:** Prompt + result
- **Implication:** What it reveals or changes
- **Action:** How to reuse the pattern
- **Look Ahead:** Which contexts need tone-switching?
